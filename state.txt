Key Georgia Programs for Families
Georgia Medicaid Waivers
Georgia offers several Medicaid waiver programs specifically for children with disabilities. These waivers help cover home and community-based services.

NOW/COMP Waiver
For children with intellectual or developmental disabilities

GAPP (Georgia Pediatric Program)
Private duty nursing for medically fragile children ages 5-21

Katie Beckett Waiver
Allows children with disabilities to qualify for Medicaid based on their own income

1-866-322-4260

Georgia Department of Human Services (DHS)
DHS administers programs that provide financial assistance, food stamps (SNAP), and other support services for families.

TANF (Temporary Assistance for Needy Families)
Cash assistance for families in need

SNAP (Food Stamps)
Help with grocery costs

Child Care Assistance
Subsidized child care for working families

Georgia Department of Behavioral Health
Provides mental health and substance abuse services, including support for children with behavioral health needs.

For children birth to age 3 with developmental delays or disabilities. Provides therapy, family training, and support services.
