Key Federal Programs
SSI (Supplemental Security Income) for Children
SSI provides monthly cash payments to families of children with disabilities. This money can help cover basic needs like food, clothing, and shelter.

Who Qualifies?
Child must have a physical or mental condition that seriously limits activities
The condition must have lasted or be expected to last at least 12 months or result in death
Family income and resources must be within SSI limits
How to Apply
Call Social Security at 1-800-772-1213 or visit your local SSA office. We can help you gather medical records and complete the application.

Medicaid (Federal)
Federal Medicaid provides health coverage for children with disabilities. In Georgia, this includes GAPP and other waiver programs.

Who Qualifies?
Child must meet disability criteria
Family income requirements (varies by program)
Some programs use the child's income only, not family income
How to Apply
Apply through Georgia Gateway or contact your local DFCS office. We'll walk you through the process and help with documentation.

1-877-423-4746

Social Security Disability Benefits (Child's Benefits)
If a parent receives Social Security Disability or retirement benefits, their child with a disability may qualify for benefits based on the parent's record.

Who Qualifies?
Parent must be receiving Social Security Disability or retirement benefits
Child must be unmarried and under age 18 (or 19 if still in high school)
Child with disabilities may qualify for benefits beyond age 18 if disability began before age 22
